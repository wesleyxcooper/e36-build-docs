## How to Read "Outer Protrusion"

Every mm this number goes up = 1mm closer to your fender liner at the wheel center.
You already touch at full lock on bumps. Safe zone to stay near or below ~87mm.

| Reference | Size | ET | Outer Protrusion |
|---|---|---|---|
| Your 034 ZTF-LP01 | 19x9.3 | 38 | **80.1mm** (baseline) |
| OEM Supra 3.0 front | 19x9 | 32 | 82.3mm (+2.2mm) |
| Safe zone ceiling (est.) | — | — | ~87mm (+7mm) |

## Porsche Macan 4-Pot Caliper — 18" Clearance Notes

**Source:** [killerbrakes.com B8 S4/S5 Macan kit fitment chart](https://www.killerbrakes.com/product/audi-a4-a5-s4-s5-b8-brake-kit-porsche-macan-brembo-4pot-dba-345x30mm-slotted-discs-new/) — community-sourced wheel compatibility list for B8 A4/S4/A5/S5 with 95B Macan 4-pot calipers on 345mm rotors.

- **Confirmed minimum: 18"** (can fit some 17" depending on spoke design)
- Someone ran 17" OEM wheels over 345mm Macan calipers with ~5–7mm clearance (worked, looked awful)
- **APEX SM-10RS 18x10 ET30**: Explicitly 3D-laser-scanned and optimized for BBK clearance. **Best confirmed choice** for 18" with Macan calipers
- **⚠️ Konig Heliogram 18x9.5 ET35 — caliper clearance is UNCONFIRMED.** The Heliogram is not on the killerbrakes.com tested list. Inner protrusion math is also concerning: the Heliogram extends **85.7mm inward** from the mounting face vs. 69.3mm for the confirmed-fit Konig Rennform 18x9 ET45. That's 16mm further into caliper territory. Separately, the **Konig Hypergram** 18x8.5 ET43 (similar Konig design language) was tested and **did not clear** even with an 8mm spacer. Treat Heliogram + Macan calipers as unverified until someone confirms it — use the SM-10RS 18x10 ET30 if Macan caliper clearance is a priority on 18".
- For 19" SM-10RS: no clearance concern — you noted this yourself

## 5. Tire Width Guide

Current: **255/35-19 on 9.3" rims**. Here's how tire sizing changes as you go wider:

| Rim Width | 255 Tire | Better Sizing | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9.0"** | ✅ Fine | — | Slightly narrower than design width but works well. |
| **9.3"** (current) | ✅ Ideal | — | Your current OEM-matched setup. |
| **9.5"** | ✅ Fine | 265 optional | 265 fills the sidewall more cleanly — adds ~5mm contact width each side. 255 is not a stretch. |
| **10.0"** | ⚠️ Stretched | **265 min, 275 ideal** | 255 on a 10" rim pulls the sidewall inward ~12–15mm per side. Reduces cornering grip and stresses the bead seal over time. **Go to 265 or 275 on a 10" wheel.** |

> **Protrusion + tire width note:** The protrusion numbers in this document are for the **wheel rim**. A wider tire extends beyond the rim. Going from 255 to 275 adds roughly **10mm of additional tire bulge per side** beyond the wheel's calculated protrusion. If you're near the rubbing limit on wheel protrusion, a 275 tire on a 10" wheel will demand ~10mm more fender clearance than a 255 on the same wheel — factor that in.

## SCCA Autocross — Street Touring Classification & Mod Legality

**Sources:** 2025 SCCA Solo Rulebook (January edition, Section 14); SCCA Classification Assistant (scca-classifier.com); JHM Motorsports 187mm crank pulley install notes; Audizine 3.0T crank pulley thread.

---

### Class: B Street Touring (BST)

The B8.5 S4 is classified in **BST** (formerly **STU** — renamed for 2025). BST is defined as: *"Primarily mid-to-high power and performing sports cars, coupes, AWD sedans, and hatchbacks."*

**BST limits (AWD car):**
- **Tires:** 295mm max section width
- **Wheels:** 11.0" max width
- **Differentials:** May substitute one (front, rear, or center) with an aftermarket mechanical LSD

Your current 034 ZTF 19×9.3 ET38 + 255/35-19 V601 setup is fully within class limits.

### Crank Pulley — Detailed Ruling

The **2025 rules removed the old prohibition** that previously said *"supercharged cars may not change the effective diameter of any pulley which drives the supercharger."* That clause is gone.

**2025 rules now say:**
- **14.10.C.5:** "Supercharger pulleys and belts of the same type as standard may be replaced with alternate pulleys allowing drive ratio changes."
- **14.10.H:** "**Any crankshaft damper or pulley may be used.** SFI-rated dampers are recommended."

Both the supercharger pulley (on the blower itself) and the crank pulley are now explicitly permitted to be changed in BST.

#### Block Grinding — Which Sizes Require It?

| Pulley Size | Block Nub Grinding Required? |
|---|---|
| 179mm (034 / JHM) | ❌ No |
| 183mm | ❌ No |
| 187mm (JHM HD Overdrive) | ❌ No |
| 207mm (JHM OAD / IE) — dual-pulley equivalent | ✅ **Yes** — JHM explicitly notes "block clearance is required" |

Per Audizine forum consensus and JHM product documentation: **179–187mm pulleys install without any block modification.** The block nub only interferes with the 207mm pulley diameter. Since the 207mm would also spin the supercharger significantly beyond its designed efficiency range (risk of longevity and reliability issues), it's not the target size anyway.

**Recommendation:** 187mm JHM HD Overdrive or 034 179mm for a single-pulley Stage 2 setup — both are bolt-on and BST-legal.

#### Stock Diameter and Fluidampr Options

**OEM crank pulley: 162mm** (two-piece rubber-isolated damper — the isolator is a known failure point on high-mileage/boosted 3.0T engines).

All aftermarket solid billet pulleys (034, JHM, IE) eliminate the OEM rubber isolator. This is a safety benefit, but they offer no viscous damping. The iABED/Fluidampr unit adds proper viscous harmonic damping.

| Option | Diameter | SC Overdrive | Damping Type | Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| OEM | 162mm | Baseline | Rubber isolator (failure-prone) | — |
| **034 179mm** | 179mm | +10.5% | Solid billet, no damper | ~$200 |
| 183mm (various) | 183mm | +13% | Solid billet | ~$200 |
| JHM 187mm HD Overdrive | 187mm | +15.4% | Solid billet | ~$220 |
| **iABED Fluidampr (stock)** | 162mm | None | ✅ Viscous damper | $699 |
| **iABED Fluidampr + 187mm ring** | 187mm | +15.4% | ✅ Viscous damper | ~$998 |
| iABED Fluidampr + 189mm ring | 189mm | +16.7% | ✅ Viscous damper | ~$998 |

**Smallest upgrade over stock:** The **034 179mm** is the mildest power increase (fewest cells per square inch over stock). If you specifically want Fluidampr, the **187mm ring** ($299 add-on to the $699 base) is the only mild overdrive option — there is no 179mm or 183mm Fluidampr ring. The 187mm Fluidampr + ring total is ~$998 vs ~$200 for a solid billet 179mm.

**Practical take:** For autocross, 179mm solid billet is fine — the runs are short, heat cycling is minimal, and the OEM rubber isolator risk is already eliminated. The Fluidampr is more valuable on a high-mileage street/track car that sees sustained RPM loads.

### Exhaust — What You CAN Do in BST

Full cat delete is off the table. Your legal exhaust options:

1. **Headers/downpipes** — Any exhaust manifold and downpipe combination is allowed (14.10.D). JHM or similar downpipes are fine as long as they don't relocate cats.
2. **High-flow cats in OEM position** — Replace OEM cats with 100+ cell/in² units located within 6" of original exit. This is the standard "Stage 2 legal" approach. APR, AWE, or similar high-flow units qualify.
3. **Cat-back exhaust** — Unrestricted downstream of the cats. Any muffler/tip/resonator configuration is legal.

The "test pipes up front + JHM catted pipes downstream" concept is specifically not legal — it separates the cat function from the original location requirement.

---

### PCV Relocation — Why It's Illegal

Section 14.10.C bans modification or removal of PCV valves that are in the air intake flow path. The Merc Racing PCV relocation kit reroutes the PCV system to prevent oil vapor from contaminating the intercooler/intake — a desirable real-world mod, but it constitutes a functional relocation of the PCV system. Run it on the street; remove it for events if you expect scrutineering (regional events rarely check, nationals may).

### If You Add Test Pipes + PCV Relocation — Class: SSP

Running test pipes and/or PCV relocation bumps you out of BST into **Super Street Prepared (SSP)**. The B8.5 S4 (2013–2016) is listed in the SSP Appendix A (confirmed in 2016/2017 SCCA Appendix A as "S4 (2010–16) & S5 (2013–17)"; classification is stable and unchanged for 2025).

**Why SSP allows what BST doesn't:**
- **15.10.F:** "Emission control devices may be modified or removed." — full cat delete, PCV relocation both legal ✅
- **15.10.I:** "Exhaust manifolds and muffler systems are free." — test pipes, any headers, any downpipes legal ✅

#### What You GAIN Moving BST → SSP

| Modification | BST | SSP |
|---|---|---|
| Test pipes / full cat delete | ❌ | ✅ |
| PCV relocation (Merc Racing) | ❌ | ✅ |
| **DOT R-compound tires** (NT01, RE71RS, A7R, etc.) | ❌ (200TW min in Street) | ✅ |
| Wheel width | 11.0" max | Unlimited |
| Fender flares (for wider rubber) | ❌ | ✅ |
| Aftermarket standalone ECU (e.g. Motec, AEM) | ❌ | ✅ |
| Weight reduction (A/C delete, spare delete, rear seat) | ❌ | ✅ |
| Unrestricted intake/induction | Mostly ✅ | ✅ fully |
| Any headers/downpipes | ✅ | ✅ |
| Supercharger pulley changes | ✅ | ✅ |

**The biggest competitive gain in SSP: R-compound tires.** NT01, RE71RS, Yokohama A7R, Falken RT660 — 200TW-class rubber transforms corner grip. This is a larger lap time delta than any engine mod.

#### What SSP Still Does NOT Allow

- Supercharger hardware changes (no porting, boring, swapping the blower) — §15.10.C.4.a
- Camshaft changes (not listed as an SP engine allowance → prohibited)
- Engine overbore beyond 0.020" — same as BST
- Adding/changing turbo or supercharger unit — prohibited

Since you're already in SSP with test pipes + PCV relocation, these are now on the table without further class penalty:
