### Bolt Pattern Conversion — 5×114.3

> **Context:** Converting to 5×114.3 opens the entire JDM wheel market (Rays, Work, Enkei, SSR, Volk, Advan) at significantly lower cost than equivalent 5×120 options. The 5×114.3 pattern is drilled and tapped M12×1.5 — same thread as OEM. Hubs retain the BMW 72.6mm center bore. The rotor hat clearance holes must also be re-drilled or replaced with pre-drilled units because the lug bolts pass through both the wheel and the rotor hat.

**M3 hub PN:** `31226761487` — differs from non-M `31226757024`. Verify against your spindle before ordering any conversion hub.

## Drivetrain — Differential Upgrade  [DRIVETRAIN]

> **Background:** Stock US E36 uses a **188mm ring gear** housing. Known weaknesses: cast aluminum diff cover ears crack under hard launches/track use; single front mount bolt is a documented failure point at high power. The comment about "weak housings and limited ratios" is accurate — the 188mm is fine at stock power but marginal for 300+ whp sustained track use. A 350z diff swap onto the E36 has **no documented adapter kit** — that's Nissan S-chassis territory (S13/S14). The proven E36 path is the **210mm big case swap** from the E32 750i or E34 M5.

### Onboard Compressor — DIY vs. Branded Kit

> The Nuke branded onboard kit **is** a VIAIR 485C Gen.2 + 2.5 gallon VIAIR tank — identical hardware used in air suspension (bags) builds. The Nuke 90C was deliberately designed at the 6–12 BAR range to be compatible with standard air suspension compressors. DIY saves ~$650 over the Nuke-branded kit.

| Component | Cost | Notes |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Nuke branded onboard kit** | **€1,341 (~$1,450)** | All-in: VIAIR 485C + 2.5 gal tank + Nuke pneufit fittings pre-configured. No sourcing required. |
| **VIAIR 485C OBA system** | **~$705 MSRP** | Same compressor + tank as Nuke kit. Missing Nuke-side pneufit quick-connect fittings. |
| **Pneufit/BSPP adapters + petcock valve** | **~$50–100** | 1/4" BSPP→NPT adapters + petcock ball valve. Source from Nuke Performance or hydraulic supplier. |
| **DIY total** | **~$750–800** | Savings of ~$650 vs. Nuke kit. |

**Practical notes for this build:**
- **Draw:** 23A max — dedicated fused circuit required (20A+ inline fuse). Same wiring approach as air suspension.
- **Tank capacity:** 2.5 gal @ 200 PSI is sufficient for the E36 (~1,100–1,200 kg with 07K swap). Approximately 5–8 full lift cycles per charge before pressure drops below the 6 BAR minimum.
- **Refill time:** ~75 sec (VIAIR 485C, 165→200 PSI).
- **Mounting:** Compressor + tank mount to roll bar, trunk floor, or spare wheel well. Tank footprint: approximately 607×151×193mm.

The Porsche 996 path above (BR0013) uses the same Brembo casting family as the 350Z/STI/EVO era 4-pot. If you have 350Z or STI calipers on hand, measure caliper mounting ear spacing center-to-center. If it matches the 996 at 130mm, BR0013 brackets will likely work — verify before purchase.

> **Verdict for this build (M3 spindles, drift/track, 17" target):** E38 Brembo + Epytec (~$400–600) is the most common upgrade in the E36 community for this spindle and the best value. Porsche 996 Brembo + BR0013 (~$500–800) is the M3 spindle-specific engineered option and the clearest path if you want that casting family. Wilwood #140-8797 ($2,414) if you want new off-the-shelf with no used parts sourcing.

## Wiring Architecture — Firewall Bulkhead Connector  [ELECTRICAL]

> **Inspired by:** Evoluto F355 restomod (MoTeC M150 + Deutsch AS circular connector engine harness). The core concept: the entire engine harness terminates at a single circular connector mounted on the firewall. Cabin side stays permanently wired; engine side unplugs as one unit with the motor.
>
> **Build relevance:** This is particularly compelling for a two-swap build (M52 → 8HP, then M52 → 07K). A single firewall bulkhead connector means the 07K harness (built offline in Phase 2) plugs directly into the same cabin-side socket as the M52 harness, with zero cabin wiring touched at swap time.

### Connector Options

| Type | Manufacturer | Pin Range | Key Trait | Source |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Deutsch Autosport (AS series)** | TE Connectivity | 1–128 pins | US de facto standard. Integrated knurl ring (no separate backshell needed). 500 mating cycles. 7 keyways (color-coded to prevent mis-mating). | [racespeconline.com](https://racespeconline.com/collections/deutsch-as-series) |
| **Souriau 8STA** | Souriau / Esterline | 1–128 pins | Cross-compatible with Deutsch AS on shared planforms. Better availability on non-standard keyways. Supports Size 8 contacts (45A) — useful for high-current circuits. Preferred by professional harness builders. | [racespeconline.com](https://racespeconline.com/collections/souriau-8sta) |
| **MIL-Spec (MIL-DTL-38999)** | Amphenol / Cannon | 3–61 pins | Larger and heavier than AS/8STA for equivalent pin count. Requires separate backshell. More cost-effective at lower pin counts. | [peigenesis.com](https://www.peigenesis.com/en/amphenol-connectors/amphenol-mil-dtl-38999-connectors.html) |

> ⚠️ **"MIL-Spec" is often misused** — connectors marketed as "mil-spec" in the budget automotive market are typically not to any defense standard. Genuine Deutsch AS or Souriau 8STA are the correct choice for motorsport.

### Contact Sizes (Deutsch AS / Souriau 8STA)
| Size | Current Rating | Wire Range | Typical Use |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Size 20** | 7.5A | 20–24 AWG | Sensor signals (TPS, CLT, MAP, crank, cam) |
| **Size 16** | 13–20A | 16–20 AWG | Injectors, solenoids, small relay coils |
| **Size 12** | 23A | 12–14 AWG | Fuel pump, ignition coil power, GPO outputs |
| **Size 8** | 45A (Souriau only) | 8 AWG | High-current power feeds |

### Sizing for This Build

A **47-way** connector comfortably handles the full M52 or 07K engine harness signal count. A **79-way** gives room for expansion (additional sensors, 07K 5th cylinder, flex fuel, wideband, lambda, turbo speed, etc.) without needing a second connector.

**Recommended approach:**
- Mount a **flanged bulkhead 47- or 79-way Deutsch AS (or Souriau 8STA)** at the firewall during Phase 1 chassis prep, while the firewall is accessible
- Wire cabin side (ECU CMC connector, fuse block, power relay board) to the bulkhead — this side is permanent
- Build M52 engine harness to terminate at a mating plug — use this through the entire M52 phase
- Build 07K harness offline (Phase 2) using same pinout → same bulkhead → drop-in swap at Phase 3
